Methods of Exposure

Ten linguistic methods that expose distortions in translations and return to the Hebrew original.

🔪

Etymological Surgery

We cut through the translation and reach the three-letter root. We show what the word meant originally, before the system disguised it.

𐤀

Paleographic Expertise

We break the word into Paleo-Hebrew letters and read it as a sequence of images. The meaning of the word is embedded in its form.

🕸

Comparative Semitology

We compare the Hebrew word with related words in Arabic, Aramaic, and Akkadian. We reveal the common semantic core.

📐

Morphological Exposure

We show that the grammatical form of a word carries meaning that translation loses. Binyan, number, gender, tense — this is theology.

📏

Syntactic Dissection

Hebrew word order carries meaning. Emphasis. What comes first is what matters. Translations smooth out the order, losing the accents.

🔍

Semantic Reconstruction

We show all meanings of the root in the Tanakh. Not just one dictionary meaning. All of them. In different contexts. We restore the full picture.

⚙️

Functional Identification

We show that a word in Hebrew describes not a nature but an action. Not "who" but "what it does." The system turned functions into beings.

🖼

Imagery Mapping

Hebrew words are rooted in physical reality. They smell, they sound, they have weight. We return the flesh to the image.

⛓️

Translation Chain Disruption

We walk the entire chain: Synodal → Slavonic → Latin → Greek → Hebrew. At each step, we show what was lost.

🔗

Smikhut Diagnostics

Smikhut is fusion. Two words become one. The system breaks the smikhut, creating false distance. We restore the unity.
